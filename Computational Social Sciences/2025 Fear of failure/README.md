# 🌍 Culture, Environment, and Fear of Failure

---

## Research Question

To what extent is human culture shaped by **social and environmental conditions**?

This project investigates how macro-level factors such as:

- economic development
- health conditions
- environmental risks

influence cultural attitudes, specifically **fear of failure**.

---

## 📊 Data

The analysis is based on the **Ecology-Culture Dataset (ECD)**:

- **220 countries**
- **1950–2020 time span**
- ecological variables (GDP, disease, etc.)
- cultural indicators (fear of failure, values, well-being)

---

## ⚙️ Data Analysis Pipeline

- data cleaning and filtering
- exploratory data analysis (distributions, correlations)
- visualization of relationships between variables
- statistical modeling

---

## 🤖 Modeling Approach

### Linear Regression (OLS)

The core model of the project is a **multiple linear regression (OLS)**:

[
\text{Fear of Failure} \sim \text{GDP} + \text{Life Expectancy} + \text{Disease} + \text{Unemployment}
]

👉 This allows us to estimate the **independent effect of each variable** on cultural attitudes.

---

## Hypotheses

- 📉 Higher GDP → lower fear of failure
- ❤️ Higher life expectancy → lower fear of failure
- 🦠 Higher disease threat → higher fear of failure
- 📉 Higher unemployment → higher fear of failure

---

## Key Results

- Economic and health factors are **strong predictors of cultural attitudes**
- Environmental constraints (e.g. disease) play a significant role
- Cultural traits appear **systematically linked to structural conditions**
